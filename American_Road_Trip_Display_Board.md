# Great American Road Trips

## Display Board Plan

### Subtitle
Discover the most famous, longest, shortest, and most memorable driving routes in the United States.

---

## Introduction
American road trips are a symbol of freedom, adventure, and exploration. Across the United States, famous highways and scenic byways take travelers through deserts, mountains, forests, coastlines, and historic towns. This display board introduces some of the best-known and most interesting American road trip routes.

---

## 10 Facts About American Road Trip Routes

### 1. Most Famous Route: Route 66
Route 66 is the most famous American road trip route. It originally connected Chicago, Illinois, to Santa Monica, California, and became a symbol of adventure and freedom.

### 2. Longest U.S. Highway: U.S. Route 20
U.S. Route 20 is the longest road in the United States Highway System. It stretches from Massachusetts to Oregon.

### 3. Longest Interstate Highway: I-90
Interstate 90 is the longest Interstate Highway in the United States. It runs from Seattle, Washington, to Boston, Massachusetts.

### 4. Shortest Interstate Highway: I-878
Interstate 878 in New York is one of the shortest signed Interstates in the country, measuring only about 0.7 miles.

### 5. Most Scenic Coastal Route: Pacific Coast Highway
California’s Highway 1, often called the Pacific Coast Highway, is famous for dramatic ocean views, cliffs, beaches, and landmarks such as Big Sur.

### 6. First Transcontinental Automobile Road: Lincoln Highway
The Lincoln Highway was the first road designed for automobiles to cross the United States. It connected New York City and San Francisco.

### 7. One of the Most Visited Scenic Routes: Blue Ridge Parkway
The Blue Ridge Parkway is one of the most visited scenic drives in America. It is especially popular for its mountain views and colorful fall foliage.

### 8. Most Dramatic Desert Route: Highway 163
Highway 163 through Monument Valley is famous for its striking desert scenery and towering red rock formations.

### 9. Historic Southern Route: Natchez Trace Parkway
The Natchez Trace Parkway follows an ancient travel corridor once used by Native Americans, traders, and settlers.

### 10. Cross-Country Road Trips Show America’s Variety
A long American road trip can pass through many different landscapes, including cities, plains, deserts, mountains, and coastlines.

## Visitor Trivia Game

### Game Title
**Guess the Road Trip Route!**

### How to Play
Visitors read the clues and guess which American route is being described.

### Trivia Clues
1. **I am the most famous American road trip route.**  
   **Answer:** Route 66

2. **I run along the California coast with beautiful ocean views.**  
   **Answer:** Pacific Coast Highway

3. **I am the longest Interstate Highway in the United States.**  
   **Answer:** I-90

4. **I am known for mountain scenery and colorful autumn leaves.**  
   **Answer:** Blue Ridge Parkway

5. **I was the first transcontinental road for automobiles.**  
   **Answer:** Lincoln Highway

### Score Guide
- **1–2 correct:** Road Trip Beginner
- **3–4 correct:** Highway Explorer
- **5 correct:** Road Trip Expert
